About

I am a Distinguished Professor of Physics and Astronomy at the University of Texas at San Antonio.

I am a leader in the design, development, and operation of observatories, telescopes, focal-plane instruments, and data systems for the American, European, and South American astronomical research communities. I have served on various international governance and review boards as well as held positions in the American Astronomical Society and the International Astronomical Union.

From 2019 - 2024, David was the Dean of the College of Sciences at UTSA.

From 2008 - 2019, he  was Director of the National Science Foundation's National Optical Astronomy Observatory (NOAO), the U.S. national observatory for night-time optical-infrared astronomy. NOAO developed, built, and operated astronomical telescopes, instruments, and data systems in Arizona and Chile. During his directorship, NOAO and its partners implemented the Community Science and Data Center (home of the Astro Data Lab), the U.S. Extremely Large Telescope Program, the Dark Energy Survey (DES), and the Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI). In 2020, NOAO assets were merged into the NSF's National Optical Infrared Laboratory, NOIRLab. David co-led the team that developed the baseline concept and implementation plan that was approved by NSF.

Previously, David held positions with the Thirty Meter Telescope Project (Pasadena/CA), the European Southern Observatory (Garching bei München/Germany), and Kitt Peak National Observatory (Tucson/AZ).

David grew up near Albany/NY but has lived most of his adult life in the border lands of Arizona, California, and Texas, a rich mélange of Anglo, Mexican, and East Asian cultures. He has extensive international working and living experience, most notably nine years in Germany as well as many extended periods in Chile. He is married to Dr. Paula Christianson-Silva, DNP, a Family Nurse Practitioner and clinical faculty in the School of Nursing at UT Heath San Antonio. David and Paula have two adult child, and enjoy hiking, running, cycling, and skiing.
